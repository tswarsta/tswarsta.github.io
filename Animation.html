Home Energy Score
Client Project

Many homes, specifically in Pennsylvania are not energy efficient. Our goal for this project - encourage home owners to obtain an energy audit.



My final piece is a video intended on being shared through social media, on websites, etc. It is meant for home owners who are unaware of problems in their home; basically it is meant to serve as a starter video for home owners, a taste of energy efficiency that will lead users into wanting to learn more about being more efficient.


Process

Post-it notes showing my initial research and beginning storyboarding ideas

Before I decided to make a short video I had to do a lot of outside research to fully understand the idea of “upgrading” ones home, the hesitations users have with doing so and what “normal” homes in Pittsburgh were like. After my initial research, I began to storyboard my idea - I wanted to target those who were self conscious enough to upgrade their homes because of social pressure, as well as those who wanted to better their neighborhood.


Post-it notes showing a early iteration of how I was going to layout the video and my reasoning for doing so

In this iteration I clearly label with what is going to be portrayed (pink), the main points (blue) and I also have begun to research specific statistics that I will utilize in my video (yellow). At this stage of the project I think that I had a good idea of what we are meant to be doing — promoting a audit without scaring the user. I also think I understood more of what the reason for the audits are, however, at this stage I still had to do research into the home energy score and how to rework it.


My final home energy score

The design that I ended on utilized the house icon I used throughout my video. I decided to target users by creating an emotion for their home - a sad home meant not energy efficient.
