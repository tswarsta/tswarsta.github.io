CONTEXT

This exhibit featured a collection of work produced by Carnegie Mellon University’s Design Class of 2018. Assemblage is a reflection of the long-term effects of our decisions and how those decisions affect the larger environment around us. We work through our processes while considering aesthetics and form to go beyond the components of a traditional design education. In each piece, the designer’s presence is balanced with the message of the project, whether prompted in studio or self deﬁned.

VIEW EVENT PAGE -
millergallery.cfa.cmu.edu

STRATEGY

Our class of 42 first divided into groups based on interest, these groups consisted of branding, curation, build, budget and catering and set up. I was in the branding team, which consisted of twelve individuals; together we created the visual identity for the showcase. We worked closely with the curation team to make a strong cohesive visual system that was consistent with the intent behind the showcase.

DELIVERABLES

The branding team was tasked with created all the deliverables for the show, which consisted of — promotional posters, banners, social media graphics, way finding systems, labels, maps and pamphlets.

We broke into smaller teams to complete the tasks once we had a consistent visual identity that we were happy with. I worked on the beginning branding visualization and word mark, posters, way-finding systems, labels and map.

PROCESS

The branding team held meetings twice a week. During each meeting, we would work in smaller teams to begin ideating the concept for the word mark as well as brand identity. My smaller team of four presented the concept, “Assemblage” as well as the beginning schematic diagrams of the design process that worked as the starting point for the identity.

At times it was difficult to work in such a large group as sometimes we had to wait for approval from all groups as it is our show and the ideas of every student had to be incorporated in order to have an effective show.
