Fruit Packaging
Graphic/ Product Design

For this project we were placed into pairs and were tasked with designing cohesive fruit packaging for one another. My partner and I selected a kiwi and a grapefruit.



Our final product took form as a acetate bag with two trays containing the fruit housed within


My partner and I’s inspiration came from the literal essence of the fruit. We scanned the fruit as well as took photos of sliced fruit on a light board to come up with our “print”, which is of a grapefruit and a kiwi.



The trays had our fruit design printed on them with a acetate casing over them that also featured the prints


After we completed our packaging we designed a zine to go with it. The zine included more playful context shots as well as my partner and I interacting with the finished design


Process


Process showing the transition to digital


My partner and I were inspired by the idea of fruit being placed on a surgical table, which is why we started with photographs of the fruit on a light board, this however, seemed to far from appetizing so we moved to printing making with the sliced fruit and eventually moved to the digital.


Iterations showing the transition from typical prints to our final halftone version


My partner and I were unsatisfied at this point as our iterations seamed to be dated, we questioned the definition of a traditional print or pattern at this point and began to focus on a single fruit. We began to manipulate this, which resulted in our end result.



The Colors of Phipps
Graphic Design/ Print

Inspiration and photographs taken at the nearby Phipps Conservatory - how can we use color to tell a story and to change the mood of an environment?



The finish product is a three by 3 inch per square booklet


I wanted to utilize color to transport the viewer into a different environment. Phipps has many different rooms with plants from many different parts of the globe with many different temperatures. I wanted to take the user on a journey through temperatures and climate by using the lines and color of plants to tell my story.


Process shows iterations of transition from plant to plant as well as the colors selected for their backgrounds


I began by sifting through the images I had taken at a walk through Phipps. The first thing that I was drawn to were the interesting or severe angles some of the plants had - this is where I got my inspiration for the transitions from. I placed them side by side to see if they could create some kind of organic flow from plant to plant.

Process showing the integration of chalk pastel


After being satisfied with the transitions I moved into blending the background and foreground. I didn’t want there to be abrupt shifts from the gradients I selected in the back and the plants in the front - this is where I utilized chalk pastel to fuse the two.
