Lets go Shopping

This project was our first UI/UX research project. We were tasked with creating a cultural probe to retrieve information. With the information we obtained we began to develop an app that would help users shop in a smarter way.



A cultural probe is a method designers use to collect information from users in a more “interesting” way. This process of creating a probe allows designers to understand what information they hope to get better as well as forming a relationship with users. For this cultural probe my partner and I wanted to learn more about why users frequent specific grocery stores and what causes users to pick certain grocery stores over others.




The cultural probes were given to five different individuals who were living and working in Pittsburgh. From the information we obtained, we found that users were willing to travel to more than one grocery store as well as travel longer distances to retrieve speciality items. From this we wanted to develop something that would assist users to “shop smarter” - what if the item they are looking for is actually more available and in stores that are closer to the ones they are currently going to?






My partner and I decided to find an app that would help users find specialty products they were looking for with more ease. With the “Lets go Shopping” app users would be able to search for a specialty project resulting in a map highlighting to the user where the closest store to them that carries the product would be.
