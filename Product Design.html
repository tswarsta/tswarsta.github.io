My Water Carrier

This project emphasized the filling, traveling and pouring of a water carrier.



During the ideation phase of this project, we as a class began by exploring primitive forms in order to discover the most optimal way of gripping an object. This project was done throughly through desktop and handcrafted model making. After exploring several different grips I decided that a simple clasp was the most interesting due to its simplicity and strength. My final design is meant to be carried and gripped under the arm - the reason for this is I regularly place my water bottle there so I can have both hands free, which is unlike the common cylindrical form. This new shape is angled, which allows the user to snugly fit it under their arm.


If I were to further iterate on this project, I would focus more on the actual volume the bottle would be able to hold. Because of the sharp incline at the top, my design does not allow the deliverable to ever be fully filled. The sharp angles are also custom to my body, how can I further this design so it can be adapted and utilized by others? The Lamp

The main focus of this project was to manipulate and alter a space through lighting design.




I was personally assigned to create a more social environment. For my lighting engine I focused on the idea of people surrounding a camp fire. I determined that a more round form was more welcoming to users as angles could be more aggressive. My lamp is meant to be stationed in the center of the table, allowing viewers to gather around and socialize.
